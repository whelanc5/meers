Artist Statement

I search for meaning by creating art in collaboration with the qualities and limitations of the physical. Through handling, observing, manipulating, listening, sensing, and responding to materials, I explore connections between the self and the external world. I try to capture internal, intangible experiences which inform spiritual understanding. My process is to construct pieces, combining intuitive, kinesthetic mark making and material handling with an overall vision that transforms as I respond to a work in progress. Evidence of construction is purposefully prominent, resulting in pieces which feel transitory rather than fixed. I strive to let the essence of each material have a voice within the creation of the work. I am interested in examining memory, longing, desire, time, entropy, transformation, love, and capturing the preciousness of acts by the human hand. Through books, prints, multi-media installations, and video and sound pieces, I offer sensory, bodily experiences and invite viewers to visit their own internal spaces. Much of my work tries to activate the viewer’s body by creating an environment they can navigate and explore physically and psychologically.

Bio

Meera Mittal (she/they) is a printmaker and book, paper, multi-media, and interdisciplinary artist. Mittal has an MFA in book arts and printmaking from the University of the Arts in Philadelphia, PA. She has a Bachelor’s of Social Work degree from Colorado State University in Fort Collins, CO and an Associate of Fine Arts degree from North Seattle College in Seattle, WA. Mittal is one of the founding members of the SPACE (Share Public Access for Creative Endeavors) Coalition. She and the other founding members curated the exhibition makeSPACE in Gallery 224 at the University of the Arts, coordinated a panel discussion about accessibility in the arts, and organized and taught zine workshops at the Free Library in Philadelphia, PA. Mittal has shown her work at the Arronson Gallery at the University of the Arts and the Icebox Project Space in Philadelphia, PA, and Phantom Realms Gallery, Columbia Bank Neighborhub, and North Seattle College in Seattle, WA. She created album artwork and contributed lyrics for various projects for Awaz Arts in New York City, NY, and performed with Chez Cherie, also based in New York City, in An Evening of Musical Triptychs and Live Printmaking. She interned at Torchluss Panik in Mexico City, Mexico and in the book and paper studios at Penland School of Craft in Penland, NC. Mittal has taught workshops in bookbinding and papermaking, participated in various art fairs, and has curated multiple exhibitions. Mittal currently resides in Philadelphia, PA.
